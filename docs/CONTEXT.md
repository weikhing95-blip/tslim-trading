## 6 — The craft: kasut manek

### 6.1 What it is
**Kasut manek** (also kasut manik, "shoes of beads") — Peranakan/Nyonya beaded slippers. The defining feature is a hand-beaded **vamp** ("muka" / face) attached to a shoe sole. Beads are tiny faceted glass seed beads (~1–2 mm). The community context is the **Baba Nyonya / Straits Chinese** culture, concentrated historically in Melaka, Penang, and Singapore.

**Heritage timing:** A surprisingly recent form. Adrian Cheah and Wikipedia date the recognisable craft to **early 20th century Malaya**, peak in the **1930s**. The defining fine cut-glass beads are themselves a 19th-century European invention. So the craft has heritage weight but is roughly **a century old as a distinct form**, not ancient.

**Status today:**
- **Singapore Intangible Cultural Heritage Inventory — ICH-044** ("Nyonya beadwork and embroidery"), dated **April 2018, updated March 2019**, category Traditional Craftsmanship. This is the **strongest formal designation** that exists for the craft specifically.
- The **kebaya** (the garment associated with kasut manek) was inscribed on UNESCO's Representative List of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity in **2023** as a joint nomination by Malaysia, Brunei, Indonesia, Singapore, and Thailand. **Kasut manek itself is NOT UNESCO-listed.** Do not claim UNESCO heritage status for the shoes; the safe and accurate claim is *"associated with the UNESCO-inscribed kebaya tradition"* and *"on Singapore's ICH inventory as ICH-044."*

### 6.2 Materials
- **Beads — "manek potong":** Tiny, multi-faceted glass seed beads, ~1–2 mm, sizes 11/0 to 15/0 (smaller number = bigger bead).
  - **Historically (pre-WWII):** imported from Europe — chiefly Bohemia (modern Czech Republic, especially Jablonec nad Nisou — world's largest bead exporter by 1928), Venice, southern France (rocaille).
  - **Today:** original manek potong are essentially extinct in commerce. Modern artisans use **Czech charlottes** (single-faceted seed beads) or **Miyuki Japanese seed beads** (15/0 preferred for fine work).
- **Base fabric:** 16-count Aida cross-stitch cloth, stretched on a frame so each bead corresponds to one grid square.
- **The frame — "pidangan":** A laced-up rectangular wooden frame holding the canvas under tension.
- **Sole:** Leather (premium) or fabric. Assembled separately after the vamp is finished.

### 6.3 Process
1. **Pattern.** Sketched on graph paper, then reduced and transferred onto Aida.
2. **Bead selection.** Sorted by hand under daylight. Peranakan palettes are chosen to *age* — colours deepen rather than fade.
3. **Stitching.** "One bead, one stitch." Worked from the centre outward — main motif first, then border, then background fill. Knots avoided on the reverse to prevent discomfort.
4. **Lasting.** Beaded vamp removed from the pidangan, shaped over a wooden last, lined, and stitched to the sole.
5. **Finishing.** Heels (or flat), sole, edges.

A "Peking knot" stitch is sometimes referenced (Patricia Choo Collection / Malaysian Reserve).

### 6.4 Time, beads, and labour economics
- Simple design: **3–6 days** at 5–8 hrs/day.
- Standard pair: **2 weeks – 1 month** (Malek Lim Penang case, Patricia Choo Collection).
- Intricate / cut bead: **up to 1 month**.
- Premium manek potong: **up to 2 months**.
- High-end documented examples: **Robert Sng (Singapore) ~100 hrs/pair**; **manekNya BeSpoke ~240 hrs/pair**; **Raymond Wong's men's commission: 18 months, 54,000+ beads**.
- **Bead count per pair:** typically 10,000–20,000; some references go higher.
- **Labour economics:** Robert Sng's pricing (~SGD $1,000 / 100 hrs) ≈ **~S$9.50/hr** before materials — below Singapore's living wage. The category survives on tourist trade, cultural-revival waves, and cross-selling.

### 6.5 Motifs & symbolism
- **Floral:** peony (wealth/honour), rose/carnation (European), hibiscus, lotus, chrysanthemum (longevity).
- **Fauna:** phoenix (bride) + dragon (groom) for weddings; butterfly (love); mandarin ducks (fidelity); goldfish (abundance); qilin (auspicious); swan, lion, chicken also documented.
- **Colour:** red = prosperity (wedding); gold = wealth; pink/multicolour = everyday joy; black/white/blue = mourning (with simple geometric patterns).
- **Modern motif innovation:** Patricia Choo Collection (Penang) incorporates George Town vintage Peranakan tile motifs. Kenny Loh × Malaysia Airlines used diamond motifs inspired by Nyonya kuih.

### 6.6 Why the craft is endangered
1. **Bead supply** — original European manek potong essentially gone; substitutes require international sourcing.
2. **Aging artisans, weak succession** — Penang's named masters mostly 70+ (May Lim Siew Seng 77, Tan Kok Oo, Khor Ewe Hock); Robert Sng 70+. Genuinely young new entrants are rare — Joel Teh (22) and Jocelyn Teh (23) in Penang are cited as *"very likely the youngest"* Nyonya beaded shoemakers.
3. **Hours-vs-price economics** — see §6.4.
4. **Demographic taste** — *"deemed untrendy by millennials nowadays"* (Sonusgear).

## 7 — Products, pricing, production

### 7.1 Categories
Standard taxonomy across the kasut manek field; TS Lim covers most of these:
- **Wedding kasut manek** — heaviest beadwork, full phoenix/dragon or paired-bird motifs, red/gold, often closed-toe; heirloom tier.
- **Festive / daily** — colourful patterns for CNY, birthdays, gatherings.
- **Mourning** — black/white/blue, simple geometric.
- **Kasut kodok** — flatties with upturned toes, traditional.
- **Kasut sepit** — slip-on / mule (cross-strap, open-toe).
- **Kasut tutup** — closed-toe; more formal, more beadwork surface, higher price.
- **Modern formats** — 2-inch heels, wedges, block heels, cross-strap sandals.

TS Lim explicitly offers: peep-toe, closed-toe, with/without heels, mules/sandals. He also offers **repair / resoling** for worn beaded shoes (preserves the original muka).

### 8.2 Marketplaces
- **Shopee MY/SG, Lazada MY** — variable quality, RM 250–800 typical.

### 8.3 Price ceiling — the unspoken cap
**~SGD $1,000** is the unspoken online retail ceiling for a contemporary bespoke pair. **MYR 1,250** is the highest fully-transactional SKU on a self-serve site (Colourbeads). Vintage and Raymond Wong–scale commissions go higher but are not click-to-buy. **There is open white space above SGD $1,000** for a configurator-driven, click-to-buy bespoke heirloom tier.

### 8.4 Gaps in the market — strategic openings
None of these are currently being done well; each is a viable differentiation lever for TSLIM Trading.

1. **Self-serve international checkout for bespoke** — every premium seller routes through WhatsApp/phone. No smooth flow that takes foreign card + measurements + binding lead-time SLA.
2. **Live-preview customisation configurator** — motif × palette × silhouette × bead type.
3. **Story-driven content on product pages** — provenance card next to the buy button ("*This pair: 14,000 Czech-cut beads, sewn by [named artisan] over 11 weeks*"). Our Grandfather Story's "Bead by Bead" mini-doc is the gold standard for the *content*, but it doesn't sit on a shoppable page.
4. **Transparent international shipping** — only Colourbeads and Your Beaded Shoes openly publish worldwide shipping with lead times.
5. **Sizing UX** — printable foot-tracing PDF, video sizing guide, try-pair / first-fit policy. Most sites cap at EU 39.
6. **Virtual try-on / AR** — completely absent from this category.
7. **Craft video on product pages** — "see *your* motif being beaded" weekly updates for an in-progress commission.
8. **Matching shoe + bag set SKUs** — beaded clutches exist (manekNya, Kenny Loh, Rumah Bebe) but no bundled product.
9. **Wedding as a first-class destination** — phoenix/dragon palette presets, his + hers pairing, dust-bag/box presentation, monogramming, certificate of provenance.
10. **Named-artisan provenance** — none of the sellers name *the individual who made your specific pair* on the product page.
11. **Bead-tier transparency at checkout** — Miyuki vs Czech vs revival rocaille filterability.
12. **Lifetime repair / re-bead / re-sole service** — table stakes for SGD $1,000+ pieces elsewhere; absent here. **TS Lim already does this informally** — turning it into a productised service is low-hanging fruit.
13. **English-first heritage education** — most makers communicate in mixed BM / Mandarin / English. Clean English-first heritage content (citing ICH-044, Baba Nyonya Museum, etc.) is wide open.
14. **Photography & brand at editorial / luxury grade** — no kasut manek seller has Aesop/Hermès-grade photography.

### 8.5 Unoccupied positioning
The single sentence that captures the strategic white space, synthesised from the audit:

> **"Internationally shippable, configurator-driven, story-rich heirloom kasut manek at SGD 800–2,500 with named-artisan provenance, video craft updates, and a true wedding tier."**

That archetype currently does not exist online. TSLIM Trading's heritage assets (the 30+ year practice, the rare both-design-and-bead claim, the 2012 awards, the no-photo design-protection ethic) align unusually well with that positioning.

## 9 — Constraints & sensitivities

These are non-obvious; they will shape product, photography, and copy decisions.

1. **No-photography policy in the shop — not a constraint for this project.** TS Lim historically refused photography in the workshop because competitors copied his designs. The user (son) has confirmed this is **not a constraint for the e-commerce build** — as family, he has direct access to commission and publish photography. Mention in copy is optional and can be reframed as part of the design-protection story rather than as an operational restriction.
2. **Production capacity is genuinely limited.** ~70 pairs/month pre-pandemic, fewer now. The site cannot promise "in stock — ships tomorrow" volumes. **Default model: made-to-order / pre-order**, not ready stock.
3. **The maker is in his mid-60s and succession is open.** The daughter's plans are uncertain — user's sister may take over the craft (updated 2026-06-14). Do not use "no successor" framing in public copy. The family's own stated stance on involving children in the business (Parvez Shaikh, 2018) should be treated as historical context, not a current position. Production is still capped at ~70 pairs/month by Lim's own hands — any e-commerce strategy requiring significant volume scaling remains misaligned with that reality.
4. **Two names to disambiguate from in marketing:**
   - **Colourbeads Enterprise** (different Lim family, 84 Jln Tun Tan Cheng Lock, Melaka).
   - **Wah Aik Shoemaker** (Melaka's bound-feet shoe specialist — different craft entirely).
5. **The Teochew-Chinese-running-a-Nyonya-craft angle** is rare and authentic. It's a story-worthy detail that should be handled with care: it positions Lim as someone who carried the craft *across* community lines through his mother's teaching, not as an outsider appropriating it.
6. **The wife's role.** Susan is a co-owner and active beader. The current site erases her. The user should make a deliberate choice about whether to surface her — there are reasonable arguments either way (focus on the master vs. honour the partnership) but the omission should be intentional, not accidental.
